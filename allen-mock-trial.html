OUR PLATFORM · SIX COMMITMENTS

We're not promising a better club.
We're building one.

Most mock trial campaigns hand-wave about "more practice" and "team spirit." We wrote a platform document. Here are the specifics — the same six commitments we submitted to advisors, now public, trackable, and linked directly to the tools on this site.

01 / 06

Grow the bench.

Cross-club partnerships (DECA first), announcement slots every week, flyers in every English classroom, and a referral track. A deeper bench means a stronger A-team.

  • DECA co-social
  • Weekly announcements
  • Referral credits
02 / 06

Train like it matters.

Role-specific curriculum (witness, attorney, opener/closer) with Quizlets, recorded scenarios, and a mentor assigned to every new member. Attorney workshops monthly.

  • Role curriculum
  • Mentor per rookie
  • Attorney workshops
03 / 06

Scrimmages that happen.

A custom scheduler other schools actually want to use. Post dates, match opponents, confirm in one click. No more DMs lost in group chats.

  • Public scheduler
  • One-click confirm
  • Calendar export
04 / 06

Fund the future.

Booster-club status and corporate sponsorships so the club isn't one fundraiser away from disaster. Tiered sponsorship (Gavel / Jury / Bench / Chambers).

  • Booster status
  • Sponsor tiers
  • Attendance-based fees
05 / 06

Specialized officer roles.

Not just President + VP. Director of Training, Director of Scheduling, Communications Lead, and Alumni Liaison — so no one person is the single point of failure.

  • Clear swimlanes
  • Documented handoffs
  • No silos
06 / 06

Outlast us.

Everything we build — the training, the scheduler, the playbooks — transfers with the role. This club should be stronger in five years, not just this one.

  • Owned domain
  • Documented SOPs
  • Knowledge transfer

CURRICULUM

A curriculum,
not a vibes-check.

Every role has a module. Every rookie gets a mentor. Every scrimmage ends with a debrief. The stuff we learned the hard way — written down.

MODULE 01

Witness preparation

Staying in character under cross. Reading the affidavit like a primary source. Timing objections. Delivered in six sessions, reinforced with live scrimmages.

Attorney workshops

Monthly sessions with practicing attorneys. Direct, cross, opening, closing — real feedback, not platitudes.

Case Quizlets

Linked Quizlet decks for every case year, every affidavit, every piece of evidence. Studiable on the bus.

Mentor pairing

Every new member paired with a returning member. Weekly 20-minute check-ins. No one learns alone.

Playbooks

Written SOPs for openings, objections, and trial prep — maintained by officers, not lost with a graduating member.

Weekly practice

Tuesdays and Thursdays, 3:30–5:00. Structured agenda posted 24 hours in advance, every time.
