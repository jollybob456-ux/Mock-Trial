WHAT WE DO

Not just a club. A program.

Mock trial rewards structure — knowing your role, your evidence, and your objections cold. Here's how membership actually works, from your first practice to your last tournament of the season.

01 / 04

Role-based training

Separate curricula for attorneys and witnesses, taught in structured sessions and reinforced with live scrimmages.

  • Witness & attorney modules
  • Mentor paired to every rookie
  • Attorney workshops
02 / 04

Consistent practice

Two structured practices a week, plus scrimmages against other DFW-area schools so you're not rehearsing in a vacuum.

  • Tues & Thurs, 3:30–5:00
  • Cross-school scrimmages
  • Agenda posted in advance
03 / 04

Shared leadership

Multiple officer roles keep the club running year over year, so no single graduating class takes the institutional knowledge with them.

  • Co-president structure
  • Mentorship carries forward
  • Open to every grade level
04 / 04

Tournament circuit

A full competitive season across the DFW region, with case materials, travel, and entry fees covered by the club and its sponsors.

  • 6 tournaments this season
  • Fees & travel covered
  • Case packets provided

RESOURCES

A curriculum, not a vibes-check.

Every role has a module. Every new member gets a mentor. Every scrimmage ends with a debrief. Public resources are below — case materials are shared privately with members.

Module 01

Witness Preparation

Staying in character under cross. Reading the affidavit like a primary source. Timing objections. Delivered in structured sessions, reinforced with live scrimmages.

Attorney Workshops

Sessions covering direct, cross, opening, and closing — real feedback, not platitudes.

Case Study Materials

Study sets for every case year, evidence packet, and affidavit — shared with members only.

Mentor Pairing

Every new member is paired with a returning member for weekly check-ins.

Playbooks

Written guides for openings, objections, and trial prep, built from past seasons.

Weekly Practice Plan

Structured agenda shared with members before every practice session.

Case-specific materials (affidavits, evidence packets, this year's case file) are member-only and shared privately by officers for competitive-integrity reasons.
