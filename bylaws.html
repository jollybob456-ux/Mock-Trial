BOOSTER CLUB

Bylaws

The governing bylaws of the Allen Mock Trial Booster Club.

Executive Board

Composition

  • President, 1st Vice President (Aide to President & Club), 2nd Vice President, Secretary, Treasurer. Director, Mrs. Holloway is an extension of the Executive Board.

Eligibility

  • Must have a student in the Youth and Government Club
    • No more than two consecutive terms in the same position (exceptions by director, Mrs. Holloway).
    • District employees may not serve in financial roles.
    • No officer shall be permitted to hold more than one office position at the same time.

Term

  • One year, begins at the start of the fiscal year.
  • Exiting Executive board members may hand off responsibilities to executive board-elect members after the election if all documentation is handed over, projects are completed, and training is complete. The exiting executive board member is still responsible for the successful completion of any deliverables by the end of the fiscal year.

Vacancy

  • President appoints replacements with board approval; interim duties assumed by the board.

Removal / Resignation

  • Board members whose child leaves the club must resign.
  • Missing two consecutive meetings without notice is considered resignation.
  • Failure to fulfill duties may result in removal by majority vote of membership.

Duties (of all officers)

  • Read and understand all governing documents
  • Appoint a chair for each committee
  • Assign/designate officer ownership for each committee
  • Transact necessary business between meetings
  • Create an annual budget
  • Monitor organizational communications
  • Maintain documentation and hand it over to successors within two weeks of election
  • Retain records for a period of 10 years for audit purposes

Director(s)

  • Are consulted on all projects, activities, and any other matters concerning the Y&GBC and the Y&G Club
  • Act in an advisory role
  • Prohibited from serving as an officer or as a signatory for financial purposes

President

The President shall have general supervision of the organization in conjunction with the director(s). They shall:

  • Preside at all meetings of the organization;
  • Regularly meet with the designated campus representative regarding booster activities (regularly is defined as monthly at a minimum);
  • Resolve problems in the membership;
  • Regularly meet with the treasurer of the organization to review the organization's financial position;
  • Select an officer (usually the secretary), that is not a bank signer, as the designee to receive bank statements and review the activity and canceled checks for reasonableness;
  • Schedule an annual audit of records or request an audit if the need should arise during the year;
  • Coordinate the work of the officers and committees;
  • Ensure all objectives are met;
  • Complete the cash management training program provided by the District; and
  • Perform any other specific duties as outlined in the bylaws of the organization.

1st Vice President — Programs

Shall:

  • Preside at meetings in the absence or inability of the president to serve;
  • Oversee, coordinate, and support the committee chairs for all assigned committees. Suggested committees:
    • Fundraising
    • Hospitality
    • Parent Engagement
    • Photographers
    • Volunteers
    • Banquet / Parties
    • Senior Acknowledgements
  • Perform other specific duties as outlined in the bylaws of the organization and administrative functions delegated by the president;

2nd Vice President — Sponsorship / Fundraising

Shall:

  • Preside at meetings in the absence or inability of the president and 1st VP to serve;
  • Oversee, coordinate, and support the committee chairs for all assigned committees and fundraising. Suggested committees:
    • Community Service
    • Membership
    • Itineraries
    • Sponsorships
    • Spirit Nights
    • Website
    • Communications
  • Perform other specific duties as outlined in the bylaws of the organization and administrative functions delegated by the president;

Secretary

Shall:

  • Obtain input from the executive board on agenda topics and post the agenda before all meetings;
  • Record and present all business transacted at each meeting, in the form of meeting minutes, of all regular & executive board meetings of the organization;
  • Obtain approval of minutes and post minutes for membership review;
  • Maintain the records of the minutes, approved bylaws, and any standing committee rules, current membership and committee listing;
  • Maintain records of attendance for each member;
  • Retrieve mail from the organization's PO Box and deliver to the appropriate owner at regular intervals;
  • Conduct and report on all correspondence on behalf of the organization;
  • Perform or oversee other duties or committees as delegated by the Executive Board.

Treasurer

Shall:

  • Serve as the chairperson of the Budget and Finance Committee;
  • Receive all monies of the organization;
  • Keep an accurate record of receipts and expenditures;
  • Pay out funds in accordance with the approved budget;
  • The treasurer, along with two other Executive Board members, will be signers on booster club checks;
  • Present a financial statement of receipts and expenditures, including bank statements, bank reconciliations, and financial statements, at every meeting of the organization, and at other times when requested by the Executive Board. Copies should be available for review by the general membership within a reasonable timeframe, if requested;
  • Present a full financial report annually and file current financial reports at the end of each fiscal year with the campus principal / campus bookkeeper and the Executive Director of Finance in Administration;
  • File sales tax reports as required by the Texas Comptroller's Office (monthly, quarterly, or annually). Sales tax reports must be filed even if no sales tax was collected for the period reported;
  • File annual IRS form 990, 990-T, 990-N or 990-EZ in a timely manner;
  • Complete the cash management training program provided by the district;
  • Other specific duties as outlined in these bylaws or assigned by the Executive Board.

Mandatory Student Club Fees

Due the first two weeks of school starting:

  • Membership fee of $10.00.
  • Team T-shirt.
